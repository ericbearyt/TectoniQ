Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about treatment, tracking, and clinical data

A treatment regimen is a prescribed course of medical treatment (e.g., chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or radiation) detailed by specific cycles, drugs, dosages, and intervals. On the **Treatment Plans** tab, you can view the active protocols, start/end dates, and completion status of each cycle.

The timeline is a Gantt-style visualization showing treatment durations across different patients. Color-coded tracks indicate the type of active treatment (e.g., dark grey for Chemotherapy, light grey for Immunotherapy). It helps clinicians quickly track overlapping cycles, upcoming scans (MRI/CT), or inpatient stays.

Cancer staging describes the size of the tumor and how far it has spread:

  • Stage I: Small and localized to a single area.
  • Stage II & III: Larger tumors that have grown into nearby tissues or lymph nodes.
  • Stage IV: Advanced cancer that has metastasized (spread) to distant organs or parts of the body.

Your records are processed using the TectoniQ secure backend pipeline, converting unstructured clinical notes into structured FHIR-compliant formats. The system enforces HIPAA-compliant protocols, ensuring all patient-identifiable information (PII) is encrypted and accessible only to authorized medical staff.

For critical alerts (such as severe hematological toxicity, high fevers, or severe pain), contact the oncology triage desk immediately at x4900 or page the attending oncologist. Patient records are marked with risk levels to help identify high-risk cases.
